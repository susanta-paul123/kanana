Associations

KSC is a highly esteemed player in the global sugar trade. Our company’s reputation is bolstered by its membership with first-class international trade houses across the world. These trade houses include:

  • The International Sugar Organization (ISO)
  • World Association of Beet and Cane Producers (WABCG)
  • World Sugar Mark Organization
  • The Refined Sugar Association
Our main objectives are to:

Established in London in 1891, the Refined Sugar Association is comprised of 111 companies and organisations from around the world that are pioneers in the manufacturing and trading of refined and white sugar.

Everything but Arms European Union (EU) initiative

Kenana has been successful in delivering its sugar and ethanol exports to the European market, taking advantage of the zero tariff under the Everything but Arms initiative of the EU. Additionally, the company has a strong presence in the neighboring and regional markets.

Arab Union membership

Kenana is also a member of the Arab Union for Industrial Export Development and other institutions, which are advised by the company's respected management and team members.

International sugar organization (ISO)

The international Sugar Organization was established in 1992,  it is an international legal character, with its permanent headquarters located in the city of London. This is where the organization works through; the World Sugar Council, its administrative agenda, executive director and employees. The main duties and concerns are strengthening international relations and cooperation in regard to global sugar issues, providing adequate information on the movement of the global sugar market (issues, solutions and the new technologies that been invented) and encouraging the increased demand for sugar, especially for non-consumable purposes.

The World Sugar agreement has 72 countries in its membership, the members represent 79% of global sugar producers, 61% of the global sugar consumers, 91% of sugar exporters, and 29% of importers of sugar. Kenana's activities as a representative of Sudan in the organization: -

Sudan has sought of joining the General Agreement on Sugar since 1986, in coordination with the Ministry of Industry, the Company played a major role in supporting the government's tendency to join the World Sugar Organization. This came after the important developments at the level of the sugar industry in Sudan since the beginning of the nineties in the twentieth century, when Sudan turned into a sugar-exporting country.

KENANA has a great global reputation in the sugar trade industry and is a member of the International Sugar Bodies (The international Sugar Organization, The Sugar Association of London, and The Refined Sugar Association) and (World Beet and Cane Growers Association) in France, hence it deals with first class international trade houses in the world sugar trade. The company continued to deliver its sugar and ethanol exports successfully to European markets benefiting from zero tariff under the EU’s everything but Arms initiative. Indeed, this is beside the neighboring and regional markets.

World Association of Beet and Cane Producers) WABCG)

The World Association of Beet and Cane Producers was established in Guadalajara, Mexico) in May 1981. The founding members include 17 government organizations that are sugar beet producers and 16 producers of sugar cane.

The main objective of the WABCG lays in it is role as an international organization qualified to deal with issues and policies related to sugar beet and sugar cane. Moreover, it also has coordination, membership and observatory status in many intergovernmental commodity organizations and their organizing agreements related to the sugar industry, such as - the International Society of Sugar Technology (ISSCT), the World Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO), the World Sugar Organization (ISO), the World Sugar Research Organization (WASRO), the World Trade Organization (WTO) and (IIRB). The WABCG membership consists of a broad base of member countries of the World Sugar Organization (ISO).

Kenana joined the International Federation of Beet and Cane Producers in 1998 and has become one of the active members through its participation in the annual meetings of the Federation. 

The membership brought the following benefits to the company:

1- Exchange of the information regarding research in developing cane samples. Researchers at the Cane Research Department in Kenana participated in many important workshops that dealt with global issues and papers that reflected the experiences of the global industry.

2- The company gets advantages from the published documents of the members and the technical aspects related to the preparation of the WTO negotiations especially those sugar related.

3- Coordination in situations and negotiations that are related to sugar trade and providing opportunities for the company to explain and reflect the industry’s position in Sudan to the regional unions, especially the European group.

In conclusion, the company seeks, according to its future prospects and the 2014 ten-year plan, to strengthen and upgrade its relations with similar institutions within the International Federation of Beet and cane Producers through the exchange of information and visits, where the company hosted the -General – secretary of beet producers in the European Union, which had a positive impact, it advanced the company's activities in the European Union.

World Sugar Mark Organization

The international sugar mark is used to distinguish natural sugar, whether it is from beets or cane, in light of the growth of industrial sugar substitutes in the world.

The idea of this brand arose in 1970 through the sugar factories of fourteen countries in cooperation with the European Committee of Sugar Manufacturers, the British Authority and the World Design Council. Hence the organization's spoon with a handful of sugar logo.

The objective of the organization: -

1-Promotion of natural sugar of all kinds, white - brown - crystal - cubes.

2- Confirmation of the international mark in all the entities that use sugar (food and beverage factories i.e. all industries in which natural sugar is included)

3-Refuting the negative opinions about the use of natural sugar, which call for encouraging the use of disease-causing artificial sweeteners.

4-The quality of the product is natural sugar, free of harmful substances to humans.

5- Maintaining the status of the sugar commodity in terms of its value and quality.

6- Distinguishing natural sugar from artificial sweeteners.

Countries using the global sugar label are now more than twenty-five countries that use the global sugar label (Canada - America - Spain - South Africa - Japan.)

Kenana registration for the international sugar mark: -

Kenana joined the International Sugar Mark Organization in January 2004 to become the sole agent for the brand in Sudan and the region. It will also become the sole reference and representative of the organization in Sudan. In addition to that, Kenana have the privileges of putting the international sugar mark on its own produced sugar for (50 ,10) kilo package.

 The company’s goals were to use the brand in the following:

1-The right to use the mark in the company’s products and letters alongside the company’s brand, enhancing the brand value of the company’s sugar products.

2-It supports the competitive marketing advantage of a company in the local and global markets together. In the local market, the company can increase its market share in the industrial sector in the long term and in the direct consumption sector as well.

3-As an important promotional tool that increases consumer culture in sugar products by building a positive image of the quality of the products.

4- The mark acts as a protection for the company's products from counterfeiting and repacking by distinguishing the products in different rounds and packages.

5- The company's access to the international brand of the company came in order to benefit from the developments and knowledge of issues related to trademarks and the excellence of the sugar commodity globally.
