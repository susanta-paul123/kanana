Projects Details

Our brownfield projects are designed to optimise existing infrastructure, whether through upgrades, renovations, or repurposing. By leveraging our expertise in process optimisation, technology, and environmental remediation, we are able to transform underutilised spaces into high-performing operations. Our focus on sustainability and social responsibility means that we are able to create value while also minimising our environmental impact and contributing to the communities in which we operate.

Plant upgrade and modernisation project – Kenana site

Description and key objectives:

  • Installation of 2 high pressure boilers and STGs
  • Electrification of mills
  • Produce 7,830 MT additional sugar production per annum Produce 142,800 megawatts excess electricity per annum
  • 73,350 MT of furnace fuel no longer required to be purchased per annum
  • Initial technical and financial feasibility is currently in finalisation process

Al Sufaya fattening and meat processing – Khartoum State and Kenana site

Description and key objectives:

  • Integrated breeding, fattening and slaughterhouse in
  • Khartoum, a key market and proximity to ports for export
  • 35,000 breeding herd
  • 25,000 bulls for fattening
  • Slaughterhouse for bulls and sheep
  • The African Development Company (ADC) in South Africa have been appointed as technical consultants

Kenana Red Sea agriculture terminal – Port Sudan State

Description and key objectives:

  • New dry bulk handling facility
  • Network of conveyors (1,500 MT per hour)
  • 2 warehouses to store bagged and bulk sugar (30,000 MT and 90,000 MT)
  • 6 silos to store grains (90,000 MT)
  • Throughput volumes at 2 million MT per annum
  • Initial technical and financial feasibility reports completed
  • Seaport Innovation group, based in the Netherlands, have been appointed as technical consultants

Project Funding

  • $100 million (USD) investmen
  • Expected construction period of 16-18 months
  • Estimated IRR of 14% and NPV of $18 million (USD) at the equity level

Solar Power Plant Project

Description and key objectives:

  • Establishing a solar power plant with maximum capacity of 40 megawatts with enough storage
  • Sustainable energy aligned with United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change (UNFCCC)

Project Funding

  • $50 million (USD) investment
  • Start date: 2022
