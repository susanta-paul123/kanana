Integrated Policies

Code of conduct Comprehensive policies

Kenana Sugar Company Ltd (KSC) is a sugar-producing company established in 1974, with an ambitious vision to be a source of food and green energy. Despite its core business which is the production of refined sugar for local and international markets, KSC diversified its business model to utilize the by-products of sugarcane such as bagasse and molasses to produce human and animal commodities such as animal feed, dairy products, ethanol, bio-gas, compost, meat production and its processing, poultry, horticulture etc.  KSC is committed to participating intensively in developing the Sudanese economy by adopting creative and innovative approaches and methodologies in the sugar by-products business, which aims at achieving zero waste through creating green economies with an ultimate goal of reducing carbon emissions and positive contribution to environment, CSR and sustainability. 

Environmental Policy

Kenana has incorporated an Environmental Policy as part of its commitment to protecting and preserving the environment and being a responsible business. We are fully prepared to comply with the required environmental practices that ensure reduced wastage and pollution. What's more, meeting the necessary local and international legal and other requirements is imperative so we can continue to improve our environmental management system.

By effectively managing our internal processes, materials and people, we aim to lower any negative impact on the environment.

Our main business objectives include:
  • Investigating the possibility of reducing hazardous and toxic materials wherever possible.
  • Reducing, re-using and recycling waste.
  • Ensuring efficient energy use.

Food Safety Policy

Ensuring the highest standards of food safety for human and animal consumption is imperative at Kenana. Our Food Safety Policy ensures we can meet the required standards, rules and regulations as set out by international regulations. These include Good Manufacturing Practice (GMP) standards as well as governmental and international bodies.

Our main objectives are to:
  • Manufacture products that meet the standards of the relevant international health certificates
  • Show our commitment towards the adoption of the quality policy which ensures the highest supply chain standards
  • Ensure that all ingredients and practices are compliant with the required international food safety standards

Health and Safety Policy

As part of our Health and Safety policy, Kenana is committed to ensuring its offices, warehouses, factories, and logistical facilities remain compliant with all the necessary requirements as set out by international health and safety standards.

Our main objectives include:
  • Ensuring all our staff are highly trained and well-equipped at all times
  • Making sure that Kenana abides by the rules and regulations as set out by the relevant safety standard authorities
Meeting the required quality and assurance standards across all departments and business units

Our Quality Control and Assurance (QCA) department ensures the optimal level of quality and assurance spanning all business units, departments, and subsidiaries. Key responsibilities within the department include quality control checks of all products, services, and company inputs. Additionally, our QCA department is responsible for setting, monitoring, and benchmarking business units, departments, and processes while maintaining quality assurance across the company's processes.

Quality control checks are also performed extensively within our central laboratories, with the department responsible for the issuance of all product export certificates.

Upholding the industry standard through effective quality control and assurance checks and processes

Our Quality Control and Assurance (QCA) department comprises two main divisions. These include Quality Control and Quality Assurance.

Quality Control (QC)

The QC section of the department is responsible for upholding the highest level of quality across all Kenana products and inputs. A number of specialised laboratories exist within the department, including laboratories for sugar, animal feed, ethanol, dairy products, water treatment, civil works, foundry, fuel & petroleum, special analysis, and microbiology.

Each of our laboratories are fully equipped with the necessary instruments to perform the required tasks that help to achieve our objectives, with our highly trained laboratory staff testing for product and input quality in the respective laboratories.

Quality Assurance (QA)

Our QA section of the department takes the lead with being responsible for the setting, benchmarking and monitoring of every process, activity, and service delivered across all of Kenana's businesses and departments.
