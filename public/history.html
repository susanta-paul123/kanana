History

Sudan stands at the meeting point of Africa and Arabia,

The Nile River with its tributaries the great two rivers that run through its central area; the White Nile and the Blue Nile, with their associated rich clay soil. The sweet-toothed millions of Sudan itself form the basis of much of the economic activity of Sudan and most of the future activity currently envisaged, in terms of area, Sudan is the third largest country in Africa with a total area of about 1,861 million sq. km and 16th largest population in the world with an estimated population of about 43.85 million people of various ethnic origins, cultures, traditions and religious beliefs. It has a cultural heritage of more than five thousand years. Sudan has marvelous natural resources, ample arable land (21% land suitable for agriculture), and water resources for large-scale agriculture projects that are considered a priority sector for the variety of soil and climate that differs from one region to another.

This country is a mineral wealthy one, with an abundance of crude oil, natural gas, gold, cotton, gum Arabic, wheat, maize, sorghum, and various tropical fruits. Sudan has large cattle ranches throughout the country and exports surplus cattle.

Sudan's sugar production has steadily increased over the last few decades in the area of White Nile State in the southern part of Sudan, bordered to the north by Khartoum State, and Kordofan State by the west.

The soil in the north, south, and east is clay, sandy soil is found in the west. As for climate, the state has a dry and semi-arid climate, and has vast lands suitable for agriculture, estimated by 6.5 acre.

There are several agricultural projects called subsistence projects, and the most important crops grown in the state are cotton, corn, sesame, sunflower, peanuts, hibiscus, vegetables, and fruits.

The White Nile State is considered the incubator of the Sugar Industry in Sudan as we find institutions considered giant sugar industries in Africa and the Middle East; the Kenana Sugar Company Ltd., the White Nile Sugar Factory, and the Asalaya Factory. Whenever we speak of the sugar industry we have to mention the sugar giant industry KENANA.

Kenana is the story of a grand multinational venture that combined western expertise, Arab financial resources and Sudan’s abundant natural resources of sun, oil, and water together with readily available manpower in the mega –industrial project that turned the desert to green and transformed Sudan’s economy and allowed it to be ready for the twenty first century. 

It’s the world largest integrated sugar estate, recognized internationally as stunning agro- industrial success story constructed in less than four years between 1976 –1980, in the middle of the sub-Sahara Desert searing heat in an undeveloped area, kenana now is a shining example of a successful business in Sudan, a legend in the sugar industry and the peak of the green sources of energy.
